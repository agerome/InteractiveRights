Raining Rights

Which rights are the most popular across history?
As countries have created new constitutions, some rights have appeared more frequently than others. For example, take the right to bear arms. At points in the late-1800s, as many as 15% of constitutions contained Second Amendment-type provisions. Now, though, only six constitutions do so.

You can track the growth and decline of the various rights through the Interactive Rights Bubble Visualization.

  • Click and hold any bubble
    that interests you to see a short description of the appropriate right, as well as the number of constitutions that protect that right in a particular year.

  • To see how rights have become more or less popular over time.
    hit the “play” button in the center, or move the slider to select a particular year.
