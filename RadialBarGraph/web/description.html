The Circle of Rights

How have constitutional rights spread around the world?
In the 18th and 19th centuries, most constitutions only protected a few rights, such as the right to free speech or the right to a jury trial. Now, though, many constitutions contain a whole array of rights, including the right to health and the right to free development of one's personality.

You can explore these changing patterns through the Interactive Rights Wheel Visualization.

  • To focus on a particular category of rights,
    simply select the appropriate item from the category view on your left. Sample text from each constitution appears in the upper-left box.

  • To view how rights in that category have changed across time,
    hit the “play” button in the center, or move the slider to select a particular year.

For further information on the data, feel free to visit www.constituteproject.org
